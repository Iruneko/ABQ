WHY THIS CITY

MY FAVORITE CITY TOUR FOR BREAKING BAD FANS

Albuquerque, New Mexico is one of my favorite cities to visit because it is the heart of Breaking Bad, one of my favorite shows of all time. Touring these famous filming locations made the story feel real again. For fans, this city is more than just a place on the map — it is a chance to step into scenes that are unforgettable.

From Los Pollos Hermanos and Walt’s school to Saul Goodman’s office, Jesse’s townhouse area, the motel, and Route 66 style desert roads, every location brings back the mood of the show. If you are a fan, this is a city you should see at least once.

ICONIC LOCATIONS

Breaking Bad Tour Stops

1. Los Pollos Hermanos / Twisters

This is one of the most famous locations for Breaking Bad fans. In real life it is Twisters, but in the show it became Los Pollos Hermanos, Gus Fring’s fast food restaurant. This place instantly brings back the world of Gus, Walt, and many unforgettable scenes.

View location

2. Walt’s School

Walt’s school represents the beginning of his double life. It may look like a normal school, but for fans it has a very different feeling. It reminds us where Walter White started before his life changed completely.

View location

3. Saul Goodman's Office

Saul Goodman's office is one of the most memorable places in Breaking Bad and Better Call Saul. The colorful exterior and unusual style make it stand out right away. Fans will recognize it as the place where many tense, funny, and unforgettable scenes happened. Visiting this spot makes the world of Saul Goodman feel real again.

View location

4. Jesse’s Townhouse Area

Jesse’s place is one of the most emotional locations in the series. It reminds fans of his struggles, growth, and some of the most memorable scenes in the story. Visiting this spot makes the show feel very close and real.

View location

5. The Motel

The motel has a rough and unforgettable atmosphere that matches some of the darker parts of the show. It is one of those locations that may look simple from the outside, but fans know how much emotion and tension is connected to it.

View location

6. Route 66 / Desert Road Feel

Albuquerque’s roads, gas stations, and desert surroundings are a big part of what makes the show feel so unique. Even simple roads and open skies can make fans remember the RV scenes, long drives, and tense moments that gave Breaking Bad its unforgettable style.

TOUR TIPS

Things I Recommend

  • Start early if you want to visit many spots in one day.
  • Bring water because Albuquerque can get hot and dry.
  • Respect private homes and neighborhoods.
  • Take photos from public areas only.
  • Use Google Maps before you start driving around.
